'She++: The Documentary' Film Screening with WiC

Time: April 9, 6:00pm

Location: Tech L324

Everyone is invited

RSVP

WiC is going to host a screening "She++: The Documentary". Also, we have a panel of four speakers - Professor Brenna Argall, PhD candidate Madeline E. Smith, Masters student Natalie Murray and undergraduate Lizz Bartos - will share their experiences and lead a discussion about females in computer science, prior to the screening. Please come and join us. Here is the trailer: http://sheplusplus.stanford.edu/film/index.php

Directed by two Stanford female technologies Ayna Agarwal and Ellora Israni, she++: The Documentary (12 min: TV-14 DL) energetically proclaims 'Hello, World' after following smart, creative, and trailblazing technologists hard at work in hi-tech. This short documentary collects research and inspirational pieces of Silicon Valley's unsung heroes to galvanize us to explore our potential as 'femgineers'. As technology is becoming increasingly ubiquitous, all demographics must harness new ideas to transform and empower technology. Think of what more 'femgineers' could do.


Dinner with IBM CTO of International Standards, Heather Kreger

Time: March 4, 5:30pm

Location: Joy Yee's

Heather Kreger has worked for IBM for more than 30 years. She is responsible for the development of Software Groups International Standards strategy. She has two publications, "Web Services for J2EE, Version 1.0" and "Java and JMX: building manageable systems". What is her experience in Computer Science industry? How does she balance her family and work? What is her suggestion for female students? Please come and join us.
