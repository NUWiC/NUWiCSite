Grace Hopper Conference

Time: October 2-5, 2013

Anita Borg Institute

link: http://gracehopper.org/2013/

The Grace Hopper Conference has historically provided tools and programs designed to help industry, academia and government recruit, retain and develop women technology leaders - tools which Northwestern alone cannot readily provide. Enabling female students to attend GHC would not only provide opportunities for each individual female's enrichment (through sessions, networking opportunities, and the career and interview fair) but also would provide an experience in which female graduate and undergraduate students could bond and connect with one another outside of the classroom through the shared interest in the computing field. Attendees of GHC often leave feeling inspired and motivated, and can bring their attitude and knowledge back to campus to share. In this way, WiC aims to excite and empower its members and foster the growth of women in technology at Northwestern.

2013 Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing theme will be "Think Big. Drive Forward" They will explore trends in Medical Technology, Media & Entertainment, Software Engineering, Education Technology and the exploding market opportunities in Mobile Experiences. The conference provides rich professional development sessions wherever you are in your career: from students just starting out to mid-career technologist to senior technical women. Presenters are leaders in their respective fields, representing industrial, academic and government communities. Leading researchers present their current work, while special sessions focus on the role of women in today's technology fields, including computer science, information technology, research and engineering.

Registration: http://gracehopper.org/2013/participate/registration/
